A resume summary is a short paragraph at the top of your resume that highlights your professional value. In 2026, hiring managers expect a concise and results-focused summary rather than generic objectives. Start with your job title and years of experience. Add two or three key achievements supported by numbers. For example, instead of saying “responsible for sales,” say “increased sales by 30% in one year.” Avoid vague phrases like “hardworking professional.” Instead, focus on measurable results and industry-specific skills. Keep your summary between 3–4 sentences. A strong summary immediately tells recruiters why you are a good fit and encourages them to continue reading your resume.
