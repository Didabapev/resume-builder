Most companies use Applicant Tracking Systems (ATS) to filter resumes. If your resume doesn’t contain the right keywords, it may never reach a recruiter. Carefully read the job description and identify repeated skills, certifications, and responsibilities. Use those exact words in your resume where relevant. For example, if the job posting mentions “project management” and “data analysis,” include them naturally in your experience section. Avoid keyword stuffing. The resume should remain readable for humans. A balance between optimization and clarity is essential. Updating keywords for every application significantly increases interview chances.
