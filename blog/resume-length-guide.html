How Long Should a Resume Be in 2026?

In 2026, the ideal resume length depends on your experience level. Recruiters prefer concise, focused resumes that highlight measurable achievements rather than long descriptions.

One-Page Resume (Recommended for Most Professionals)

If you have less than 10 years of experience, a one-page resume is usually best. It forces you to include only the most relevant skills, achievements, and experience.

  • Entry-level candidates
  • Mid-level professionals
  • Career changers

Two-Page Resume (When It Makes Sense)

Senior professionals, executives, and candidates with extensive technical or academic backgrounds may use two pages. However, every line must provide value.

What Recruiters Prefer

Hiring managers typically spend less than 10 seconds scanning a resume. A shorter, well-structured resume often performs better than a long, unfocused document.

Tips to Keep Your Resume Concise

  • Remove outdated jobs older than 10–15 years
  • Focus on results, not responsibilities
  • Eliminate repetitive phrases
  • Use bullet points for clarity

Final Advice

Quality always beats quantity. A focused one-page resume that highlights achievements and relevant skills will usually outperform a longer document.
