Top Resume Mistakes to Avoid in 2026

Even qualified candidates get rejected because of simple resume mistakes. In a competitive job market, small errors can cost you interviews.

1. Spelling and Grammar Errors

Typos make your resume look unprofessional. Always proofread carefully and use grammar-checking tools before submitting your application.

2. Using a Generic Resume for Every Job

Sending the same resume everywhere reduces your chances. Tailor your resume to each job description to improve ATS compatibility.

3. Listing Responsibilities Instead of Achievements

Employers care about results. Instead of writing “Responsible for managing projects,” write “Managed 5 projects and increased efficiency by 25%.”
