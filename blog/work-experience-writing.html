How to Write Work Experience on a Resume in 2026

Your work experience section is the most important part of your resume. Recruiters look at it first to evaluate your qualifications and impact.

Use Reverse-Chronological Order

List your most recent job first. Include your job title, company name, location, and employment dates.

Focus on Achievements, Not Duties

Avoid simply listing responsibilities. Instead, show measurable results and contributions.

Weak Example: Responsible for managing a sales team.

Strong Example: Led a sales team of 8 and increased revenue by 35% within one year.

Use Action Verbs

  • Managed
  • Led
  • Improved
  • Developed
  • Optimized
  • Increased

Optimize for ATS

Include keywords from the job description naturally in your bullet points. This improves your chances of passing Applicant Tracking Systems.

Keep It Relevant

Remove outdated roles and irrelevant experience. Focus only on positions that support your current career goals.

Final Advice

A strong work experience section clearly demonstrates impact, growth, and measurable results. Keep it concise, achievement-focused, and tailored for each job.
