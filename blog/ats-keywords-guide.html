Resume Keywords for ATS in 2026

Most companies use Applicant Tracking Systems (ATS) to filter resumes before a recruiter ever sees them. If your resume does not include the right keywords, it may be automatically rejected.

What Is an ATS?

An Applicant Tracking System is software that scans resumes for specific keywords related to skills, job titles, certifications, and responsibilities. The system ranks candidates based on keyword relevance.

How to Find the Right Resume Keywords

  • Carefully read the job description
  • Identify repeated skills and phrases
  • Look for required certifications
  • Match the exact terminology used by the employer

Where to Place Keywords

Include important keywords in your resume summary, work experience section, and skills list. For example, if a job posting mentions “project management” and “data analysis,” use those exact phrases naturally in your experience descriptions.

Avoid Keyword Stuffing

Do not overload your resume with repeated keywords. ATS systems are becoming smarter and may flag unnatural formatting. Your resume must remain readable and professional for human recruiters.

Final Tips to Pass ATS in 2026

  • Use a simple layout
  • Avoid graphics and complex tables
  • Save your resume as PDF (if allowed)
  • Tailor your keywords for every job application
