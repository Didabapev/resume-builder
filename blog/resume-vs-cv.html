Resume vs CV: What’s the Difference in 2026?

Many job seekers use the terms "resume" and "CV" interchangeably, but they are not the same document. Understanding the difference helps you submit the correct application.

What Is a Resume?

A resume is typically a one-page document tailored to a specific job. It highlights relevant skills, work experience, and achievements.

What Is a CV?

A CV (Curriculum Vitae) is more detailed and often longer than one page. It includes academic background, research, publications, certifications, and more.

Main Differences

  • Length: Resume (1 page), CV (multiple pages)
  • Purpose: Resume for most jobs, CV for academic/research roles
  • Customization: Resume is tailored; CV is more comprehensive

Which One Should You Use?

In the United States and most private-sector jobs, employers expect a resume. For academic, medical, or research roles, a CV is typically required.

Final Tip

Always read the job description carefully. If the employer requests a CV, provide one. Otherwise, submit a well-optimized resume.
