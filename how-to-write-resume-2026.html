How to Write a Resume in 2026 (Step-by-Step Guide)

Writing a resume in 2026 requires more than listing your experience. Modern resumes must be ATS-friendly, well-structured, and tailored to each job. In this complete guide, you will learn exactly how to create a resume that gets interviews.

1. Choose the Right Resume Format

The most effective resume format in 2026 is the reverse-chronological format. It highlights your recent experience first and works best with applicant tracking systems (ATS).

2. Write a Strong Professional Summary

Your summary should be 2–4 sentences that explain your experience, key skills, and career goals. Keep it concise and results-focused.

You can quickly generate a professional summary using our Free Resume Builder.

3. Highlight Work Experience

List your work experience with measurable achievements. Use bullet points and action verbs such as: improved, managed, developed, increased, optimized.

4. Add Relevant Skills

Include both hard skills and soft skills. Make sure the skills match the job description.

5. Optimize for ATS

Many companies use ATS software to scan resumes. To pass ATS screening:

  • Use simple formatting
  • Avoid images and graphics
  • Use keywords from the job posting
  • Save your resume as PDF

6. Keep It One Page

For most job seekers, one page is ideal. Only senior professionals should consider two pages.
